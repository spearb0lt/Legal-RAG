The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (Act No. 35 of 2019) came into force in stages from 20 July 2020. It repealed the Consumer Protection Act, 1986.

Section 2(7) — "consumer" means any person who buys any goods or hires any service for consideration, including users with the approval of the buyer. Excludes commercial-purpose purchases (with a livelihood-by-self-employment exception).

Section 2(11) — "deficiency" includes negligence, fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in the quality, nature, and manner of performance of any service.

Section 2(47) — "unfair trade practice" includes false representation, misleading advertisement, bait-and-switch, and 11 other enumerated categories.

Sections 28-33 — Establishment of District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions ("District Commissions"). One per district.

Sections 42-46 — Establishment of State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions ("State Commissions").

Sections 53-57 — Establishment of National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission ("National Commission").

Pecuniary jurisdiction (per the 2021 Rules):
  District Commission: complaints where value of goods/services paid does not exceed Rs. 50 lakh.
  State Commission:    above Rs. 50 lakh and up to Rs. 2 crore.
  National Commission: above Rs. 2 crore.

Section 35 — A consumer complaint may be filed by the consumer, any recognised consumer association, the Central or State Government, or one-or-more consumers having same interest (representative).

Section 38 — Procedure on admission of complaint: notice to opposite party, opportunity to file written response within 30 days (extendable to 45), then evidence.

Section 39 — Reliefs available include: removal of defects, replacement of goods, refund of price, compensation, discontinuation of unfair practice, withdrawal of hazardous goods, and corrective advertising.

Section 65 — Service of notice may be by electronic means including e-mail.

Section 100 — The Act is in addition to and not in derogation of any other law.

Section 101 — Power of Central Government to make rules.

Filing channel — Most complaints can now be filed online via the e-Daakhil portal at https://edaakhil.nic.in, which is the National Consumer Commission's e-filing system rolled out across many State Commissions.
